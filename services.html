How does land appreciation work in Nigeria?
In Nigeria, land appreciates in value as areas grow and develop. For example, if new roads, schools, or businesses are built near a piece of land, its value is likely to increase. Demand also plays a big role—when more people move into an area or invest there, the price of land naturally goes up. It’s like planting a seed in fertile soil—the right location and timing can make your investment grow significantly.
What factors should I consider when investing in land?
When buying land, think of it as securing your future. Start by looking at the location—areas with good roads, nearby schools, and plans for development are goldmines. Always double-check the documents to make sure everything is legitimate and avoid issues later. Also, ask yourself: Is this land meant for building, farming, or resale? Understanding the zoning and potential use is key. Finally, go for places where infrastructure is improving—electricity, water, and road access will make a huge difference in value over time.

The ROI depends on the location, but in rapidly developing areas, you can expect your land to appreciate by 50% to 200% within 3 to 5 years. Investing in land here means you’re setting yourself up for substantial financial growth.
